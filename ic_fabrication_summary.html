ü™® From Sand to Wafers

Raw Material

Sand is 25% Silicon. Silicon is the 2nd most abundant element after Oxygen. Silicon is separated from sand and repeatedly purified to below 1 ppb impurity (Electronic Silicon Grade).

Crystal Growth Process

A silicon seed is placed in molten silicon in a crucible. The seed is rotated and pulled slowly, allowing silicon to crystallize on it, forming a cylinder of pure uniform crystal. The cylinder is then cut into wafers and polished.

Modern wafers are 30cm wide and a few mm thick.

üîß Key IC Fabrication Steps

1. Lithography

The process for pattern definition by applying a thin uniform layer of viscous liquid (photo-resist) on the wafer surface. The photo-resist is hardened by baking and then selectively removed by projection of light through a reticle containing mask information.

  • Positive Photoresist: Softens when exposed to UV light - exposed areas are removed
  • Negative Photoresist: Hardens when exposed to UV light - exposed areas stay

2. Etching

Selectively removing unwanted material from the wafer surface.

  • Wet Etching: Using liquid chemicals (Hydrofluoric Acid) - fast, simple, but isotropic
  • Dry Etching: Using ionized plasma gas - more advanced, better results, some forms are anisotropic

3. Deposition

Films of various materials are applied on the wafer. Two main processes are used:

  • PVD: Physical Vapor Deposition
  • CVD: Chemical Vapor Deposition

üîê Device Isolation

To prevent parasitic devices between neighboring transistors, isolation techniques are used:

  • LOCOS (Local Oxidation of Silicon): Older technology creating thick oxide between transistors, produces 'bird's beak' requiring larger minimum separation
  • STI (Shallow Trench Isolation): More advanced technology that relies on etching 'trenches' between devices and filling them with SiO‚ÇÇ

‚öôÔ∏è Additional Techniques

MEMS (Micro ElectroMechanical Systems)

Using Reactive Ion Etching and Deep Reactive Ion Etching, mechanical structures can be etched into silicon, resulting in 3D structures being built on the chip.

Ion Implantation

The most widely used technique to introduce dopant impurities into semiconductors. Ionized particles are accelerated through an electrical field and targeted at the semiconductor wafer.

CMP (Chemical Mechanical Polishing)

A planarization technique by applying a chemical slurry with etchant agents to the wafer surface. Usually applied between different steps in IC fabrication.

Q1: True or False

Q2: True or False

Positive photoresist hardens when exposed to UV light.

A) True
B) False
‚úì Answer: B) False

Positive photoresist softens (not hardens) when exposed to UV light, and the exposed areas are removed. Negative photoresist is the one that hardens when exposed to UV light.

Q3: True or False

STI (Shallow Trench Isolation) is an older technology compared to LOCOS.

A) True
B) False
‚úì Answer: B) False

STI is a more advanced and newer technology compared to LOCOS. LOCOS is the older technology that creates 'bird's beak' and requires larger minimum separation.

‚úì Answer: C) Wet etching with HF
